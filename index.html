About This Tool

How the Math Vocabulary Translator works, its limitations, and privacy information.

How It Works

The translator uses a rule‑based parser that recognizes common math‑word patterns. It does not use AI or external APIs; everything runs in your browser.

  • Tokenization: The phrase is split into words and phrases.
  • Pattern matching: Rules match phrases like “more than” to addition, “less than” to subtraction (with reversal).
  • Symbol mapping: Each matched pattern produces a corresponding mathematical symbol or expression.
  • Parenthesis insertion: The parser adds parentheses where needed for correct order of operations.

Limitations

  • The parser works best on standardized, textbook‑style phrasing. It may struggle with highly colloquial or ambiguous wording.
  • It currently supports single‑variable expressions; multi‑variable systems are partially supported.
  • Some calculus and statistics phrases are translated to symbolic forms rather than full computations.
  • If a phrase is ambiguous, the tool may offer multiple possible translations and ask you to choose.

Privacy

All data stays on your device. No phrases, translations, or mistake logs are sent to any server. The tool uses localStorage to save your recent translations, practice results, and mistake dashboard data. You can clear this data at any time via the “Clear All Data” button on the Mistakes page.

Open Source

This project is open‑source. You can view the code, suggest improvements, or contribute on GitHub.
